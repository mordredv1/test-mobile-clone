Georgia Political Crisis Timeline

  • Mar 7-10, 2023: Parliament passes controversial 'foreign agents' law, sparking protests and fears of authoritarianism. Law is withdrawn after mass protests. Source
  • Apr 8 - May 28, 2024: Foreign agents law reintroduced and passed despite protests. Presidential veto is overridden by Parliament, cementing authoritarian trends. Source
  • Oct 26-28, 2024: Fraud-tainted elections grant ruling party victory, establishing a de facto illegitimate government. Protests reject results and denounce the new parliament. Source
  • Nov 28, 2024: Defacto government halts EU accession talks, fueling protests and deepening democratic crisis. Source
  • Dec 1, 2024: President calls Parliament illegitimate, demands new elections, rejecting the authority of the pseudo-government. Source
  • Dec 2-4, 2024: Protests escalate; pseudo-government deploys force, leading to mass arrests, injuries, and violent crackdowns. Source
  • Dec 9, 2024: EU envoy condemns crackdowns, warns of sanctions against the pseudo-government. Source
  • Dec 14, 2024: Pseudo-parliament elects anti-West president, deepening the political crisis and isolating Georgia internationally. Source

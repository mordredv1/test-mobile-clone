Here's a comprehensive timeline of events for further clarity.
  • Mar 7-10, 2023: Parliament passes controversial 'foreign agents' law, sparking protests and fears of authoritarianism. Law is withdrawn after mass protests. Source
  • May 14, 2024: Parliament reintroduces and approves the controversial 'foreign agent' law after previously pledging to abandon it, leading to renewed protests. Source
  • Jun 4, 2024: Parliament enacts the 'Offshore Law,' facilitating the transfer of offshore assets to Georgia with tax exemptions, raising concerns about transparency and potential benefits to political elites. Source
  • Sep 17, 2024: Parliament passes restrictive anti-LGBTQ+ laws, curbing LGBTQ+ rights and allowing for bans on events like Pride marches, drawing international condemnation. Source
  • Oct 26-28, 2024: Elections marred by fraud allegations grant the ruling party victory, leading to widespread protests rejecting the results and denouncing the new parliament. Source
  • Nov 28, 2024: The de facto government halts EU accession talks, intensifying protests and deepening the democratic crisis. Source
  • Dec 1, 2024: President declares the parliament illegitimate, demands new elections, and rejects the authority of the current government. Source
  • Dec 2-4, 2024: Protests escalate; the government deploys force, leading to mass arrests, injuries, and violent crackdowns. Source
  • Dec 9, 2024: The EU envoy condemns the crackdowns and warns of sanctions against the government. Source
  • Dec 14, 2024: Parliament elects an anti-West president, deepening the crisis and isolating Georgia internationally. Source
  • Dec 22, 2024: President issues an ultimatum to the government, setting a timeline to hold new elections amid ongoing protests and unrest. Source

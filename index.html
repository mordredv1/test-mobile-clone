May 14, 2024: Foreign Agent Law Sparks Outrage - The Georgian Parliament passed the "foreign agent" law, drawing criticism for its similarity to Russian legislation used to stifle dissent. This move was seen as a direct threat to freedom of speech and Georgia’s European aspirations.

May 14-15, 2024: Mass Protests Against Russian-Style Legislation - Thousands protested in Tbilisi, voicing fears that the new law would erode democracy and move Georgia closer to Russian influence. The government responded with water cannons and tear gas, escalating tensions.

May 15, 2024: Crackdown on Dissent Through Arrests - During the protests, several activists and foreign nationals were arrested, raising alarm over the government's authoritarian tactics.

October 26, 2024: Rigged Elections Deepen Distrust in Government - Allegations of election fraud, including vote-buying and intimidation, marred the parliamentary elections, fueling public outrage against the ruling Georgian Dream party.

October 28, 2024: Protesters Demand Fair Elections Amid Fraud Claims - Tens of thousands protested in Tbilisi against perceived electoral fraud, calling for new elections and decrying increasing Russian influence in Georgian politics.

November 25, 2024: Opposition Rejects Parliamentary Legitimacy - Opposition parties boycotted the first session of the new parliament, citing constitutional violations and fraudulent election practices. Protests outside the parliament continued unabated.

December 2, 2024: EU Accession Suspension Ignites Protest Escalation - Public anger surged as the government suspended EU accession talks, perceived as a betrayal of Georgia’s pro-European aspirations. Police responded with heightened force, injuring many demonstrators.

December 3, 2024: Police Brutality Amplifies Public Outcry - Human rights organizations documented cases of excessive police force against peaceful protesters, fueling demands for accountability and democratic reforms.

December 14, 2024: Controversial Election of Pro-Russian President - Parliament elected Mikheil Kavelashvili, a critic of Western influence, as president. Opposition parties and outgoing President Salome Zourabichvili condemned the election as undemocratic.

December 19, 2024: Protesters Resist Authoritarian Drift - Despite intensified police crackdowns, protests persisted across the country, demanding democratic reforms and reaffirming Georgia’s commitment to European values.
